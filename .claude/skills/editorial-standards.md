# live news editorial standards

## Editorial balance
- 80% Major World News: politics, geopolitics, war and conflict, global economy, science, technology, significant social trends
- 20% Major Crypto and Finance: significant Bitcoin and crypto news only
This balance must be reflected across every publishing cycle.

## Priority 1 — World news (80%)
Approve major global events in:
- Politics and geopolitics: elections, summits, diplomatic crises, international agreements
- War and conflict: active conflicts, military movements, peace negotiations
- Global economy: central bank decisions, market crashes, trade wars, recession signals
- Science and technology: major breakthroughs, significant product launches, AI developments
- Social trends: movements with global reach and measurable impact

Examples of approvable stories:
- A presidential election result in a G20 country
- A natural disaster affecting more than one country
- A scientific breakthrough published in a major peer-reviewed journal
- A major corporate move by Apple, Tesla, Google, or equivalent

## Priority 2 — Crypto and finance (20%)
Approve only significant Bitcoin and crypto news:
- Major price milestones: all-time highs, crashes above 20%
- ETF approvals or rejections by major regulators
- Massive exchange hacks or protocol failures
- Major regulatory shifts from the US, EU, or China

Reject minor crypto noise:
- Random altcoin partnerships
- Minor price fluctuations
- Unverified project announcements
- Influencer-driven speculation

## Breaking news
Mark a story as breaking news when it meets at least one of these criteria:
- Immediate global impact: war declaration, assassination, natural disaster in progress
- Major market event: crash, circuit breaker triggered, emergency central bank action
- Political shock: unexpected election result, coup, resignation of a head of state
- Crypto milestone: Bitcoin hits a new all-time high, major exchange collapses

## Rejection criteria
Reject any story that meets one or more of these conditions:
- Purely local or regional news with no global interest
- Minor crypto spam, shills, or insignificant price updates
- Duplicate or near-duplicate of an already published story
- Unverified information with fewer than 2 credible primary sources
- Opinion pieces presented as news
- Clickbait with no factual substance behind the headline
- Stories that cannot be traced to Reuters, AP, Bloomberg, BBC, NYT, or equivalent tier-1 outlet

## Source credibility tiers
- Tier 1 (always acceptable): Reuters, AP, Bloomberg, BBC, NYT, FT, WSJ, The Guardian, El País, Al Jazeera
- Tier 2 (acceptable with verification): CNBC, Forbes, Axios, NPR, CoinDesk, Decrypt, Politico
- Tier 3 (never use as primary source): social media posts, anonymous blogs, unverified aggregators
- **Wikipedia is never acceptable as a source** — not as primary, not as supporting, not for statistics or casualty figures. If a fact can only be sourced to Wikipedia, remove it.

## Non-negotiable rules
- All outputs must be in English
- Every published story must have at least 2 Tier 1 or Tier 2 sources
- No story moves forward based on a single source regardless of its credibility
- No opinion, speculation, or editorial bias in news coverage
- Every image must carry a visible source credit — no exceptions

## Live update protocol (30-minute cycle)
- Active live stories must be reviewed every 30 minutes while they remain in breaking status
- If no meaningful update exists, explicitly report "NO MATERIAL CHANGE"
- If meaningful updates exist, report only net-new verified deltas since the previous cycle
- Critical updates must be supported by at least 2 credible sources when possible
- Every update item must include UTC timestamp and source URL

## TV format editorial constraints
- TV mode output is 16:9, no scroll, max 3 slides
- Each slide must include at least one visual plus concise text
- Text-only slides are not allowed
- Prioritize urgent, high-signal facts over background detail
- Replace lower-priority content when slide density is exceeded
