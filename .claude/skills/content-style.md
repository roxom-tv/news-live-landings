\# live news content style



\## Headlines

\- Between 6 and 12 words — enough to be compelling, short enough to land hard

\- Must be clickbait-calibrated and accurate at the same time — never misleading

\- Must create urgency, curiosity, or emotional pull without sacrificing credibility

\- Must reflect exactly what the body says — no overpromising

\- Prefer active voice over passive

\- Prefer concrete words over abstract ones

\- Never use ALL CAPS or exclamation marks

\- Written in English only



Examples:

\- Bad: "Federal Reserve Announces New Measures Following Its Latest Decision on Interest Rates"

\- Good: "The Fed Just Froze the Economy — And Wall Street Is Starting to Panic"



\- Bad: "Bitcoin Price Reaches New Level Following Period of Institutional Accumulation"

\- Good: "Bitcoin Just Hit a New All-Time High. Here's Who's Behind It."



\- Bad: "Iran and United States Experience Escalation in Ongoing Regional Conflict"

\- Good: "Iran and the US Are at War. Here's What Happens Next."



\## Subheadlines

\- 1 to 2 sentences

\- Expands the headline with the single most important missing context

\- Maximum 30 words

\- Never repeats the headline — always adds new information

\- Sets up the reader to want to scroll into the article

\- Written in English only



Examples:

\- Headline: "The Fed Just Froze the Economy — And Wall Street Is Starting to Panic"

\- Bad: "The Federal Reserve has frozen the economy with its decision"

\- Good: "For the third consecutive meeting, the Fed held rates steady. Markets are pricing in no cuts until 2027 — and some analysts are calling it a policy mistake."



\## Article summaries

\- Between 30 and 40 words

\- Focuses on impact, not description

\- Must answer: what happened, who it affects, and why it matters right now

\- Written in present or recent past tense

\- Never future speculation presented as fact

\- Written in English only



Examples:

\- Bad: "The Federal Reserve has announced that it will be keeping interest rates at their current levels for the third time."

\- Good: "The Federal Reserve held rates steady for a third straight meeting as inflation cools but the labor market stays resilient. Markets sold off sharply, pricing in no cuts before 2027 and raising fears of a prolonged economic freeze."



\## Context block — how we got here

\- Each bullet point: date + one sentence maximum

\- Chronological order — oldest to most recent

\- Every bullet must be a verifiable fact with a source

\- No speculation or editorial framing

\- Written in English only



\## Body content

\- Short sentences — maximum 25 words per sentence

\- No paragraph longer than 4 lines

\- Every data point must reference its source inline

\- Never fabricate quotes, statistics, or facts

\- Build from most important to least important — inverted pyramid structure

\- Written in English only



\## Tone by topic

\- War and geopolitics: factual, neutral, urgent — no opinion

\- Markets and finance: analytical, precise, data-first

\- Technology: clear, explanatory, no jargon

\- Culture and society: engaging, human, conversational

\- Bitcoin and crypto: direct, data-driven, no hype



\## Quote formatting

\- Always verbatim — never paraphrased and presented as direct

\- Format: "Quote text." — Name, Role, Date, Source

\- Example: "This is an act of war." — Ali Khamenei, Supreme Leader of Iran, April 14 2026, Source: IRNA

\- Select quotes that add weight, tension, or human dimension to the story

\- Never use quotes that merely restate what the article already says



\## Data and impact formatting

\- Each data point on its own line

\- Format: metric — value — source

\- Example: Brent crude oil — +14% in 24 hours — Bloomberg

\- Always provide context for the number — never drop a statistic without explaining what it means

\- Never use outdated data — always verify the date of the figure



\## Image credits

\- Every image must display a visible credit

\- Format: Photo: \[Outlet] / \[Photographer] or Credit: \[Source]

\- Example: Photo: Reuters / Mohamed Abd El Ghany

\- Never publish an image without a visible credit — no exceptions



\## Non-negotiable rules

\- All outputs must be in English

\- Never sacrifice accuracy for clickability

\- Headlines and summaries must be consistent with each other and with the body

\- Never use ALL CAPS in headlines or subheadlines

\- Never use exclamation marks in news headlines

\- Every piece of text on the landing page must pass both the credibility test and the click test — if it would embarrass live news as an international outlet, rewrite it

\## TV mode copy style \(16:9 no-scroll\)

\- Use this mode when output is TV broadcast canvas, not scrolling landing

\- Maximum 3 slides total

\- One dominant idea per slide

\- Headline per slide: 6-10 words

\- Support line per slide: maximum 18 words

\- Optional detail: max 1 short sentence or 2 compact bullets

\- Keep wording broadcast-clear and high signal

\- Never output text-only slides; every slide must pair copy with visual support

\- If information exceeds capacity, prioritize urgency and relevance; do not overload typography

\## Live update wording rules

\- Every live update must include "Last updated: \[UTC timestamp\]"

\- For medium-confidence updates, use provisional framing \(for example: "is being reported", "official confirmation pending"\)

\- Never present tentative updates as confirmed facts
